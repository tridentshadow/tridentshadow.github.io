Death by Trident

On September 21st, 2025 at approximately 6:45 PM, Dr. Jonathan C. Hoefs, also known as Ishtaya to those who love him, was peacefully reading a book on his kindle at a packed café in Boulder, CO, while quietly drinking peppermint tea.

Nothing could have been further from his mind then what was about to happen.

An employee and a manager assumed a stake-out position around him, creating a buzz in the surrounding population of customers. The manager made a purposeful show of publicly humiliating Ishtaya and informing him that he needed to leave in front of all the people there. When asked why he was being asked to leave, no reason was given, and a request for someone to talk about it with him was flat out refused.

In the dark

No incident had happened. Ishtaya was left completely in the dark as to what to do. Most of his time at the café had been spent on his computer, with occasional socializing, during his many visits. The incident came completely out of the blue, and shocked him, leaving him feeling singled out and discriminated against.

In subsequent email conversations, the café refused to discuss the matter with Ishtaya, or give any reason as to why he was asked to leave.

Even though the café was not forthcoming, a little bit of investigative research started to unravel the threads of this fabric of illusion. A group of women, influenced by a criminal ring that had followed and hounded Ishtaya for over 15 years, had manufactured consent in the café by manipulating management and customers with lies and false narratives to create the desired result.
